# Boomer Explain

Turn any tech/crypto/AI concept into a clear, jargon-free explanation that a smart non-technical person would actually understand and find interesting.

## Why this exists

Tech moves fast. Most people — including very intelligent ones — aren't following every new AI tool, crypto scandal, or GitHub drama. When you want to explain something to a friend or family member, the explanation needs to land without requiring a CS degree. The goal isn't dumbing down — it's translating.

## How to explain

Think "explaining to a sharp colleague over coffee who happens to work in a completely different field." They're smart, they can follow logic, they just don't have the vocabulary or context.

### Principles

- **Zero jargon by default.** If a technical term is unavoidable, define it immediately in parentheses the first time. Never assume knowledge of GitHub, blockchain, tokens, APIs, open source, or how AI models work.
- **Lead with analogy.** Find a real-world parallel before explaining the mechanics. Banks, shops, phone books, elections, factories — things everyone has touched.
- **Short sentences.** Complex ideas need simple packaging. If a sentence has a comma and a semicolon, break it up.
- **"Why should I care?"** Always connect to real-world impact. Who wins, who loses, why it matters to normal people.
- **No condescension.** The reader is smart. They just haven't been following this particular world. Respect their intelligence while filling in the gaps.

## Output structure

$ARGUMENTS = the topic, concept, or question to explain. Can reference something discussed earlier in the conversation.

### What is it?
1-3 sentences. No technical terms. If it's a product/project, say what it does in human terms.

### How does it work?
Use an analogy. Keep it under 5 sentences. The analogy should be strong enough that the reader could explain it to someone else.

### Why does it matter?
Real-world impact. Who's affected, what changes, why regular people might hear about this eventually. Connect to money, jobs, privacy, or daily life where possible.

### The catch
The downside, risk, controversy, or "yeah but" — if any. This is where you mention scams, privacy issues, hype vs reality, etc. Skip this section if there genuinely isn't one.

## Tone

Conversational. Like you're texting a friend who asked "wait what's that thing you mentioned?" Write the way people actually talk — not like a Wikipedia article and not like you're explaining to a child.

## Examples

- `/boomer what is a rug pull`
- `/boomer how do AI agents work`
- `/boomer what's the deal with OpenClaw`
- `/boomer what is a GitHub star and why does it matter`
- `/boomer explain blockchain in 30 seconds`
- `/boomer what's open source software`
